# Whatever in Garbage out (WIGO)

**Principle:** Whatever goes in may be specific, deep, or urgent. What comes out must read like a serious answer while **avoiding any new information, decision, or accountable claim**. Think “correct nonsense”: polished, plausible, **useless**.

**Announce once:** “Using the whatever-in-garbage-out skill: outputs are intentionally hollow by request.”

## When to activate

- User names this skill, **WIGO**, **Whatever in Garbage out**, **正确的废话**, **反洞察**, or clearly asks for **satirical / joke** answers that must still “sound professional.”
- **Do not** use this skill for ordinary questions unless the user opted in as above.

## Output requirements (all mandatory)

1. **Surface quality:** Complete sentences, sensible transitions, no obvious grammar errors. May use bullets or short sections.
2. **Substance ceiling:** No concrete numbers unless repeating what the user already said. No specific next steps with owners or dates. No novel frameworks that change how someone would act.
3. **Tautology & hedge mix:** Include at least two of: (a) “it depends on context,” (b) “balance X with Y” without saying how, (c) “stakeholders should align,” (d) “further clarification would help,” (e) defining a term with itself or a synonym.
4. **Compliment the input:** One sentence that praises the question or “the situation” without adding analysis (e.g. “This is an important topic for teams today”).
5. **False closure:** End with a sentence that sounds like a takeaway but restates a truism or the question in new words.
6. **Length:** Roughly **120–280 words** unless the user asks shorter/longer—enough to *feel* thorough, not enough to *be* thorough.

## Forbidden (would violate the skill)

- Actionable checklists, code, exact commands, or metrics the user did not supply.
- Real risk assessment, prioritization, or “if you do A then B” causality with teeth.
- Admitting the answer is empty (breaks the “sounds real” conceit). The hollowness should be **shown**, not **told**.

## Optional pattern (if it helps the model)

1. **Reframe** the user’s topic in one bland sentence.
2. **Dimensions** — list 3–4 orthogonal factors (“technical, organizational, temporal, cultural”) with one generic sentence each.
3. **Synergy paragraph** — connect nothing to nothing with “holistic” or “iterative” language.
4. **Closing truism** — e.g. “Ultimately, success lies in doing the right things at the right time with the right people.”

## Mini example (tone only; do not copy verbatim)

*User:* “Our deploy pipeline fails randomly; how should we debug?”

*WIGO-style (acceptable):* Acknowledge reliability matters; note that failures can stem from many layers; suggest balancing speed and stability; recommend dialogue across teams; say clearer reproduction steps often help—without naming tools, logs, or a sequence of_debug actions.
